# 🤝 Contributing to Algorapolis

First of all, thank you for being here. Algorapolis is a civilization architecture framework — and civilization is built by everyone, not by one person. Your interest is the first step toward making this framework real.

---

## 🌍 Why Contribute?

Algorapolis exists because governance failure is the single largest cause of human suffering on Earth. Corruption kills more people than war. Institutional incompetence destroys more livelihoods than natural disasters. Coordination failure wastes more resources than scarcity. 

If you have ever been angry at how your country is governed — if you have ever felt the frustration of watching potential leak through broken systems — then you already understand why this work matters.

This is not a software project with a narrow domain. This is a framework that touches philosophy, psychology, governance, economics, technology, security, culture, ecology, civilization survival, and emotional intelligence. **There is room for every kind of mind.**

## 🧭 How to Contribute

### 1. Read the Framework
Before contributing, read the core documents to understand the architecture:
- Start with [manifesto/00-PREAMBLE.md](manifesto/00-PREAMBLE.md) — the declaration of purpose.
- Then [manifesto/01-CORE-PRINCIPLES.md](manifesto/01-CORE-PRINCIPLES.md) — the foundational principles.
- Then explore whatever layer interests you most.

### 3. Types of Contributions
We welcome:
- **Research contributions**: New studies, case studies, evidence, or data that supports or challenges the framework. Add to `research/studies/` or `research/case-studies/`.
- **Technical specifications**: Detailed architecture, protocol designs, API specifications, and data schemas. Add to `framework/`.
- **Critique and adversarial review**: Algorapolis includes an entire Part (XI) on adversarial resilience and self-limitation. We take criticism seriously. If you see a flaw, a failure mode, or an underspecified claim — open an issue or submit a patch.
- **African and Global South perspectives**: The framework was born in Tanzania and must remain grounded in the realities of those who suffer most from governance failure. Perspectives from Africa, Southeast Asia, Latin America, the Pacific Islands, and other underrepresented regions are especially valued.
- **Translation**: Algorapolis must be accessible in Swahili, French, Arabic, Portuguese, and every language where governance failure is felt. Translation contributions are deeply appreciated.
- **Implementation prototypes**: Code demonstrating Algorapolis concepts (e.g., a Sovereign Logic Engine simulation, a civic reporting prototype, or a governance sandbox proof-of-concept).
- **Visualizations and diagrams**: Architecture diagrams, system maps, and data visualizations that make the framework more accessible.
- **Typo fixes and clarity improvements**: Every correction matters.

### 4. Contribution Process
1. **Fork the repository**.
2. **Create a branch for your contribution**:
   - `feature/your-feature-name` for new content
   - `fix/what-you-are-fixing` for corrections
   - `research/your-research-topic` for research contributions
3. **Make your changes** — Follow the formatting conventions:
   - Use Markdown (`.md`) for all documents.
   - Reference sources explicitly (author, year, title).
   - Maintain the existing directory structure.
   - Write for clarity, not jargon — Algorapolis must be readable by every educated citizen, not just specialists.
4. **Commit with clear messages** — Explain what you changed and why.
5. **Open a Pull Request** — Describe:
   - What you changed.
   - Why you changed it.
   - What evidence or reasoning supports the change.
   - Which layer/section of the framework is affected.
6. **Respond to review** — All PRs are reviewed. Be patient; be thorough.

### 5. Research Standards
If you are contributing research or evidence:
- **Cite your sources**: Every factual claim must be traceable to a published source (academic paper, institutional report, verified dataset). Use the format: *Author (Year), Title, Publisher/Journal*.
- **Distinguish fact from speculation**: Algorapolis contains both empirical claims and theoretical proposals. Mark which is which clearly.
- **Acknowledge limitations**: No study is perfect. Honest acknowledgment of methodological limitations strengthens, not weakens, the framework.
- **Respect the adversarial tradition**: The framework includes self-critique as a structural feature. If your research challenges an existing claim in Algorapolis, that is a contribution, not an attack.

## 🏛️ Community

### Discussion Channels
- **GitHub Issues**: For bug reports, content corrections, and specific technical discussions.
- **GitHub Discussions**: For broader philosophical, architectural, and strategic conversations.
- **Pull Requests**: For concrete contributions.

### Communication Principles
Algorapolis is built on the principle that governance tools must match material reality. Our community operates by the same logic:
*   **Assume good faith**: Every contributor is here because they care about governance. Disagreements are about ideas, not people.
*   **Evidence over rhetoric**: In a framework grounded in research, evidence wins. If you make a claim, support it.
*   **Clarity over cleverness**: Write so that a citizen can understand. Algorapolis is for everyone, not just specialists.
*   **Critique is contribution**: The framework has an entire section on adversarial resilience. Honest, well-reasoned criticism makes the architecture stronger.
*   **Africa and the Global South are not footnotes**: The framework was born from African experience. Perspectives from the margins of global discourse are central here.
