# 📜 Algorapolis Code of Conduct

## 🏛️ Preamble

Algorapolis is a civilization architecture framework built on the principle that governance tools must match material reality. Our community operates by the same logic: the tools of our collaboration — how we treat each other — must match the reality of who we are: diverse humans with different backgrounds, different expertise, different struggles, and a shared commitment to making governance better.

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces governed by the Algorapolis project: GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other forum where contributors interact under the Algorapolis banner.

It is inspired by the frameworks that Algorapolis itself draws upon: constitutional governance with enforceable protections, distributed accountability, and the principle that the system serves the people — not the other way around.

---

## 🔑 Our Standards

### We Commit To:
*   **Treating every person with dignity and respect**, regardless of their nationality, ethnicity, language, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, education level, economic status, or political views. Algorapolis is a pluralistic framework; our community must reflect that pluralism.
*   **Valuing diverse perspectives**, especially those from the Global South, from Africa, and from communities that have suffered most from governance failure. The framework was born in Tanzania; the perspectives that created it must never be marginalized within it.
*   **Engaging with ideas, not attacking people**. Disagreement is essential to the adversarial resilience that makes Algorapolis strong. Disagreement with an idea is welcome; personal attacks are not.
*   **Supporting new contributors**. Everyone starts somewhere. A confused question from a newcomer is an opportunity to build the community, not a nuisance to dismiss.
*   **Being honest about uncertainty**. Algorapolis includes an entire Part on self-limitation and humility. Contributors should model this: say *"I don't know"* when you don't know, and distinguish speculation from evidence.
*   **Making Algorapolis accessible**. Write clearly. Explain jargon. Design for the citizen, not the specialist. Governance that cannot be understood cannot be legitimate.

### We Do Not Tolerate:
*   **Harassment of any kind** — sexual, racial, religious, ethnic, national, linguistic, or otherwise. This includes slurs, stereotypes, intimidation, stalking, unwanted attention, and any form of dehumanizing language.
*   **Personal attacks** — Criticize the idea, not the person. *"This argument has a flaw because..."* is welcome. *"You are stupid for thinking..."* is not.
*   **Discrimination** — Any statement or action that treats a person as lesser based on their identity. Algorapolis architecturally prevents discrimination in governance; our community must prevent it in practice.
*   **Deliberate misinformation** — Knowingly making false claims, citing non-existent sources, or fabricating data. Algorapolis is a research-grounded framework; intellectual honesty is non-negotiable.
*   **Exclusionary behavior** — Deliberately making spaces unwelcoming to particular groups, languages, or perspectives. If Algorapolis is genuinely for all civilizations, its community must be open to all peoples.
*   **Abuse of power** — Using contributor status, maintainer access, or social capital to silence, marginalize, or intimidate others. The framework has anti-capture architecture for governance; we apply the same principle to community governance.
*   **Trolling and bad-faith participation** — Joining discussions solely to disrupt, provoke, or waste community time. Constructive criticism is valued; destruction for its own sake is not.

## 🛡️ Enforcement

### Reporting
If you experience or witness a violation of this Code of Conduct:
1.  **Report it**: Contact the repository maintainer directly via GitHub or email. All reports are taken seriously and handled with complete confidentiality.
2.  **Document what happened**: Include links, screenshots, or quotes whenever possible. The framework values evidence; so does our enforcement process.
3.  **No retaliation**: You will not face retaliation. Reporting a violation is a contribution to community health, not a disruption. Retaliation against reporters is itself a Code of Conduct violation.

### Process
All reported violations are investigated. The process follows the principles of the framework itself:
*   **Due process**: The accused has the right to know what they are accused of and to respond.
*   **Transparency**: The outcome of enforcement actions is communicated to the reporter and, where appropriate, to the community.
*   **Proportionality**: Responses are scaled to the severity of the violation, from private warnings to permanent bans.
*   **No double jeopardy**: A resolved matter is not reopened without new evidence.

### Consequences
Violations may result in:
*   **Private warning** — For first-time, minor violations. The contributor is informed of the violation and asked to correct their behavior.
*   **Public warning** — For repeated or more serious violations. The warning is visible to the community, creating accountability.
*   **Temporary suspension** — For serious violations. The contributor loses access to project spaces for a defined period.
*   **Permanent ban** — For severe, repeated, or unrepentant violations. The contributor is permanently removed from all project spaces.

*All enforcement decisions are documented and reviewable.*

## 🌍 African and Global South Sensitivity

Algorapolis was born in Tanzania. It was created from the direct observation that governance failure disproportionately harms the people least empowered to fix it. This origin carries specific responsibilities:

*   **Language diversity**: English is the working language of this repository, but it must never be a gatekeeper. Contributors who are more comfortable in Swahili, French, Arabic, Portuguese, or any other language are encouraged to contribute in their language; the community will work to translate and integrate.
*   **Connectivity reality**: Many potential contributors have limited or intermittent internet access. Our contribution process must account for this — large downloads, complex toolchains, and real-time-only collaboration are barriers. We favor lightweight, asynchronous, offline-capable workflows wherever possible.
*   **Cultural context**: Governance means different things in different cultures. What looks like corruption in one context may be reciprocity in another. What looks like efficiency in one context may be dehumanization in another. We approach cultural differences with curiosity, not judgment.
*   **Economic reality**: Many of the people most affected by governance failure — and therefore most qualified to contribute to its solution — cannot afford to volunteer unpaid labor. We acknowledge this structural inequality and do not penalize contributors who cannot devote unlimited time.

## 🏛️ Governance of the Community

This Code of Conduct is itself governed by the principles it describes:
*   **It is a living document**: As the community grows and evolves, this Code must evolve with it. Amendments are welcome through the standard contribution process.
*   **It applies equally to everyone**: The framework creator, maintainers, and new contributors are all bound by the same standards. There are no exceptions for status.
*   **It is enforceable**: A Code of Conduct without enforcement is a suggestion, not a standard. This Code is enforced as described above.
*   **It is revisable**: If any provision of this Code is found to be unjust, unclear, or counterproductive, it should be challenged and improved — just as Algorapolis itself is designed to be challenged and improved.
