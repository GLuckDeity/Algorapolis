## Studies

Each study provides an in-depth literature review and analysis of a specific domain referenced in the Algorapolis specification. Studies go beyond the specification's citations to survey the broader academic landscape, identify open questions, and propose research directions.

| # | Study | Key Questions |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 01 | Civilizational Collapse | Why do governance systems fail structurally? What patterns recur across empires? |
| 02 | Sovereign Logic Engine Design | How can computational governance remain narrow, constrained, and auditable? |
| 03 | Emotional Intelligence & Governance | Can institutional emotional intelligence prevent the cold-state failure mode? |
| 04 | Privacy by Mathematics | How do ZKPs, differential privacy, and homomorphic encryption guarantee citizen rights? |
| 05 | African Governance Context | What are the specific challenges and leapfrog opportunities in East Africa? |
| 06 | National Digital Twin | Can a nation be mirrored in real time without becoming a surveillance instrument? |
| 07 | Governance Sandbox Theory | Can policies be tested in synthetic environments before affecting real populations? |
| 08 | Offline Resilience | How does civilization maintain governance during disconnection and infrastructure failure? |
| 09 | Economic Telemetry Methods | How can mobile money, satellite data, and property signals provide real-time economic visibility? |
| 10 | Predictive Governance Ethics | How can anticipatory governance avoid self-fulfilling prophecies and authoritarian drift? |
| 11 | Cultural Preservation in Computation | How can algorithmic governance avoid cultural homogenization? |
| 12 | Transition Architecture | How do nations actually migrate to new governance operating systems? |
| 13 | Anti-Capture Mechanisms | How can constitutional architecture prevent elite capture across all domains? |

## Case Studies

Each case study provides a detailed analysis of a real-world governance experiment or failure, drawing lessons for the Algorapolis architecture. Case studies are selected to represent both positive and negative specifications — what works and what must be avoided.

| Case Study | Type | Key Lesson |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Estonia: Digital Nation** | Positive | Digital governance at scale is achievable; technical modernization alone is insufficient without institutional reform |
| **Singapore: Smart Nation** | Positive | Real-time coordination is technically feasible; the cost is concentrated institutional authority |
| **Taiwan: Civic Tech** | Positive | Participatory digital governance can resolve politically intractable questions; advisory authority limits democratic impact |
| **Dutch Childcare Scandal** | Negative | Algorithmic governance without emotional intelligence produces morally catastrophic outcomes |
| **Tanzania Election 2025** | Negative | Governance systems that provide no structural mechanism for preventing power perpetuation will be exploited |
| **China: Social Credit System** | Negative | The nightmare scenario — centralized, opaque, punitive surveillance governance |
